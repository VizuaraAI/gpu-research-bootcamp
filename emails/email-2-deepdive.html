From Diffusion LLMs to MoE:
GPU Research That Gets Published

Here are 5 of the most novel research directions from our bootcamp — each with zero existing published work.

1
TP + DP High Novelty

Parallelism-Aware DLM Training

Mercury achieves 10x faster inference on H100s, but all existing parallelism strategies (Megatron-LM, DeepSpeed) are designed for autoregressive architectures. This project co-designs TP/DP for diffusion-based language models.

Target: NeurIPS / ICML
2
EP + TP High Novelty

MoE × Diffusion LLMs

No published work combines MoE with diffusion-based text generation. Expert routing conditioned on token identity AND denoising timestep could yield the first trillion-parameter DLM trainable on current hardware.

Target: ICLR / NeurIPS
3
DP + TP High Novelty

Speculative Decoding for Discrete Diffusion Models

Combines two of ML's hottest topics. A draft denoiser predicts token masks in 3–5 steps instead of 10–100, while a verifier confirms across GPUs. No equivalent exists for DLMs.

Target: ICML / ICLR
4
PP + DP High Novelty

Zero-Bubble Pipeline Parallelism for Diffusion Training

Zero-bubble PP (ICLR 2024) assumes autoregressive models. Diffusion models have fundamentally different training dynamics. Better PP could reduce training cost of Stable Diffusion 3 and Sora by 20–30%.

Target: ICML / NeurIPS
5
All 5D High Novelty

AutoML for 5D Parallelism Configuration

For a 256-GPU cluster, thousands of valid TP/DP/PP/CP/EP configurations exist. Build a learned cost model + search algorithm to find the optimal config automatically. Could become the "NAS for parallelism."

Target: NeurIPS / OSDI / MLSys
